About the Team

We are an interdisciplinary research team dedicated to exploring the complexities of microbial interactions and their significant impact on the well-being of the holomicrobiome, all while upholding scientific integrity and fairness.

Professor and Chair of Microbiome Engineering

Sahar El Aidy

Sahar's academic journey spans multiple continents, starting with her studies in Pharmaceutical Sciences in Alexandria, Egypt, followed by her MSc and PhD in Microbiology in Wageningen, the Netherlands. After conducting postdoctoral research at University College Cork in Ireland, she held positions such as Rosalind Franklin Fellow and Associate Professor at the University of Groningen. Her current research aims to fundamentally understand how gut bacteria adapt to the dynamic gut environment and influence key aspects of intestinal function, including bowel movement, on various scales.

Markus Schwalbe

Markus Schwalbe joined the lab (initially at the University of Groningen) as a PhD candidate in January 2022. He is developing an ex vivo gut culture system to study complex peristaltic movements, as well as bacterial interactions on the mucus surface. His aim is to investigate the interplay between prebiotics, including Human Milk Oligosaccharides (HMOs), the (infant) gut microbiome, and its host through bacteria-produced metabolites, and to unravel their impact on gastrointestinal integrity and motility.
